The In Betweens

There's something afoot.

My earphones are missing. I need to run an errand.

It's not too much really. I need to buy milk from the kirana store about 500m away from me. But I'm twitching. Scrambling around the house like an addict with withdrawal symptoms.

My parents have noticed this. They point out to me that it's barely a 5min walk, and I don't need to necessarily bother much about it. They'd even gone so far as to suggest taking my two-wheeler and cut it even shorter. Scary.

Though the love of god, I just can't. Those five precious minutes could let me finish five minutes of a podcast I've been putting off for a while. Or maybe burn through a video from my Watch Later at 1.5x. Or listen to an album I've been saving for when the "right moment" finally arrives.

Within 5 minutes, I'd found it.

Now running the errand would only take 5 more.

I'd not want to be 'bored' on the way there, would I?

For a change, yesterday I went out for a walk at 5:30 AM.

Now look. I don't have the tendency to wake up this early. I just 'coincidentally' was not able to sleep because of an unceremonious fear of not being able to breathe when I have a runny nose.

With not much to do, I found myself roaming Bangalore at 5:45 AM. The city was still fast-asleep as I wandered deserted streets, beneath a sky caught in indecision, torn between two suitors: the setting moon and the rising sun. Yet again, I had to wear my earphones.

However, spring in Bangalore is different. The flowers blossom. Shades of purple, pink and yellow surround the city. They beckoned you to stare and appreciate, and howled when you didn't.

I was one such victim.

Greeted by such a wonderful tree as hues of dawn peppered the skies, I couldn't help but feel a teensy-bit ashamed of myself. Did I choose to ignore this beautiful world in order to listen to something I'd forget a few minutes later?

Did I sacrifice boredom serendipity for digital gratification?

I stared upon the tree for an answer. It seemed 'real'. It invited me to experience. It invited me to feel, to partake in it's blossoms. But is this what influencers, YouTubers and all the pros advice you to do — embrace boredom?

No wonder I'd keep using my phone if they'd use such a dreary word, boredom, to describe an emotion so euphoric.

These random moments of serendipity progressively keep getting rarer and rarer. In that moment we tend to accept, embrace and enjoy it. We cherish it after it passes. But seconds later we're back on to listening something.

The initial silence is unsettling, unfamiliar and foreign to us. Yet, as we come around to it, this feeling seems warm, seems friendly and seems 'real'.

We'd expect such moments to return, yet we never build our surroundings to welcome such moments. It might be the terminology that puts you off.

For with all your knowledge, you'd describe this as being bored. But at that moment, you're experiencing bliss (Ānanda). You're experiencing the environment. The air you inhale, the trees that blossom, the setting moon's wistful gaze and the rising sun's triumphant shine. You're not bored, you're experiencing something 'in-between'. What would you call it?

A sensory experience?

No! too crude.

A sensual experience?

NOOO! Too vulgar.

It's just a moment in-between.

So, when are you going to slow down to let your cherry blossoms find you?
