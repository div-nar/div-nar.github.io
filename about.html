About

I'm Divith. I work out of the Founder's Office at Dognosis, doing everything relevant to keep us afloat. The work sits at the intersection of product, ops, and the peculiar chaos of a team that's still becoming itself. But it's a welcome challenge.

I did take the long road to come here. I started off as an analyst assembling data at the Indian School of Business, before being blessed with an amazing mentor and an opportunity to work at August AI, where I learnt what high standards really are.

However, I soon found myself also bit with the research bug. With AI, the world was my oyster. So I did what any rational being would do and splurged it on research. From building AI-focused trading systems that placed bets on AI-driven bull runs, to working on the relevance of linguistics in understanding LLMs better, I'd like to be an open book, as long as I can afford the tokens.

My attempts at research were vindicated as I found myself a part of Lossfunk. To share the stage with the best in the country as a part of a residency was a pleasure I still can't process yet. Here, I had LLMs play against each other, all on Age of Empires IV. Calling it a fever dream would be putting it lightly.

I write at kwerky, a newsletter about India's business, cultural and economic culturosphere, with the sole intention of making one ask more questions than before. The kal-chakra keeps spinning, and no resistance offered ceases its movement. But to aid its flow is an act of worship. So I keep doing it through my pen.

I run long distances, I drum, and I hike to collect rocks.

Find me at kwerky and @div_narendra.
